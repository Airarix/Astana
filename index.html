Astana

History

Astana is the capital of Kazakhstan and a city of modern development. Founded in 1830 as the Akmola fortress, it gradually became an important administrative center.

Since 1997, Astana has been developing as the capital of the country. Today, it is a modern city with new districts, distinctive architecture, and a symbol of progress.

The city continues to grow, combining its historical background with modern technologies. Astana creates a comfortable urban environment and remains a center of national significance.

Geography and climate

Astana is located in northern Kazakhstan, in the steppe zone, along the Ishim River. The city lies on flat terrain, which contributes to its sharply continental climate, with cold and windy winters and warm, dry summers. Large temperature differences and low precipitation are typical of the region.
